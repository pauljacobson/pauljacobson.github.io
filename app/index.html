My Journey To Code

This isn't my first career by any means. It isn't even my first passion but I've wanted to become a developer for decades.

When I became unemployed in April 2017, I decided to make good use of my time at home, in between applying for content marketing positions and taking on freelance writing work. I decided to go back to the beginning and learn front-end/web development.

I started with HTML, which I was already fairly familiar with, and moved on to CSS, which I really enjoyed. There is something about HTML and CSS that really appeals to me. I think this is because with HTML and CSS, you can *see* the changes happening just by refreshing your browser window.

It started with a birthday

Of course, as I learned, I had to put my new knowledge to use. I started with a little site for my wife to wish her a happy birthday.
